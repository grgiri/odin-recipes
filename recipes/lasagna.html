Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked dish featuring alternating layers of flat, wide pasta sheets, savory sauces (typically ragù and béchamel), and cheeses like mozzarella and Parmesan

Originating from Emilia-Romagna, it is commonly prepared as a rich, layered casserole, known for being a satisfying, bubbly, and savory comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Butter for greasing
  • 2 ½ cups Ragù
  • 1 pound fresh mozzarella torn into 1-inch pieces
  • 3 hard-cooked eggs finely chopped
  • 2 cups bechamel
  • 2 cups finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • Sfoglia all'Uovo cut into 4 sheets measuring the size of your baking dish

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375ºF. Grease a 9- x 13-inch baking dish with butter.
  2. Spoon a ½ cup layer of ragù on the bottom of the baking dish. Place a pasta sheet on top. There's no need to cook the pasta in advance. It will cook in the juices of the ragù.
  3. Spoon another ½ cup of ragù over the pasta. Distribute ¼ of the mozzarella, ¼ of the chopped eggs, ½ cup of bechamel, and ½ cup of Parmigiano-Reggiano evenly over the tray.
  4. Repeat the pasta, ragù, mozzarella, egg, béchamel, and Parmigiano-Reggiano layering three more times.
  5. Layer the remaining ragù, mozzarella, bechamel, and Parmigiano-Reggiano over the final pasta layer.
  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes
  7. Uncover the baking dish, then continue baking until the edges of the pasta are curled up and browned, 30 to 40 more minutes.
  8. Allow the lasagna to rest for about 30 minutes before serving in slices.
